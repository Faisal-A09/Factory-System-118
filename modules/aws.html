// 01 — INTRODUCTION

Three utilities, one facility

This facility runs on three engineering systems that look unrelated but share one trait: the plant cannot operate safely or properly without any of them. The RO system handles water treatment and the supply of drinking and washing water. The firefighting system protects life and assets. The compressed-air system produces clean, dry air to drive the pneumatic equipment on the production line.

// 09 — TAKEAWAY

One facility, three specialized utilities

The facility is built around three independent utility systems, each with a clear and separate job.

RO System
Treatment, drinking & washing water.

From the NWC source through four tanks and the RO line, it delivers safe drinking water and reliable washing water — while protecting the membranes that make it possible.

Firefighting System
Emergency water for fire protection.

A dedicated tank and a layered pump sequence keep firefighting water available at all times — even if power fails — focused on life safety and asset protection.

Compressed Air System
Clean dry air for the pneumatic line.

A two-stage screw compressor, with backup and emergency units, supplies filtered and dried compressed air at stable pressure to keep production running.
